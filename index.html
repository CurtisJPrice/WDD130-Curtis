Eagle Creek, Oregon

Eagle Creek, Oregon, is an unincorporated community named after the local stream, which was likely named for the eagles that frequented the area. The community was established in the mid-1800s, with a post office opening in 1867. Philip Foster, a prominent figure in early Oregon history, played a key role in the area's development, establishing a farm and mill. Eagle Creek is also known for its access to the Eagle Creek Trail, a popular hiking destination in the Mount Hood National Forest.
